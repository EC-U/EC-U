        <p style="background-image: none;margin: 0px;padding: 0px;box-sizing: border-box;">
         Tim Pelton is an Associate Professor in the Faculty of Education at the University of Victoria, Canada. He teaches undergraduate classes in mathematics pedagogy and educational technology, and graduate classes in educational technology and research methods. His research interests include: development, validation and application of measurement instruments; the creation and evaluation of educational apps; the application of appropriate technologies to enhance the teaching and learning process (e.g., iPads, GPS, 3D printing, coding etc.); and the use of creative apps on mobile computers to engage students in problem solving, communicating, reasoning and representing their understandings through virtual manipulatives, games, comics and video artifacts. Prior to earning his PhD in Instructional Psychology and Technology at Brigham Young University, Tim had earned a BSc and MSc in Computer Science, and a BEd in Secondary Mathematics Education at the University of Victoria. Tim has 9 years experience as a computer programmer (developing CAD and GPS software), and 10 years experience as a teacher (teaching math, science and technology in middle and high school).
